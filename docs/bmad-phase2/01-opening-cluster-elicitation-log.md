# GameAIgents Opening Cluster — Elicitation Log

_Date: 2026-03-18_  
_Status: Completed challenge pass for opening cluster_

## Overview
This log records the advanced elicitation pass used to pressure-test the opening Product Brief cluster.

## Elicitation 01 — First Principles
**Question:** What must be true for GameAIgents to deserve to exist?  
**Finding:** It must solve a workflow gap that existing tools still underserve: moving from fuzzy intent to editable engine-native continuation, not merely creating impressive demos.

## Elicitation 02 — Anti-Wedge / Reverse Assumptions
**Question:** What happens if we position it as “AI game development for everyone”?  
**Finding:** The product immediately becomes too broad, too hype-prone, and too close to crowded prompt-to-game competitors. Broad category language weakens strategic credibility.

## Elicitation 03 — Pre-Mortem
**Question:** Why would the product fail even if the demo looks strong?  
**Finding:** Because users would discover the output is brittle, not truly editable, hard to continue in-engine, and overpromised relative to what the system can reliably ship.

## Elicitation 04 — Red Team vs Blue Team
**Red team:** “This is just another AI game generator with fancier copy.”  
**Blue team response:** Only if the opening language stays abstract. It becomes differentiated when it promises structured artifact flow, deterministic scaffold generation, and engine-native continuation.

## Elicitation 05 — Comparative Analysis Matrix
**Question:** Which opening emphasis better separates the product from competitors: generation speed, world-model spectacle, or continuation value?  
**Finding:** Continuation value wins. Speed and spectacle are easier for competitors and incumbents to match. Structured continuation is harder and more defensible.

## Elicitation 06 — Cross-Functional War Room
**Participants simulated:** product, technical architect, indie creator, GTM, trust/compliance.  
**Finding:** All parties aligned that the opening must avoid “full game” language. Product wants aspiration; engineering needs buildability; trust/compliance needs honesty; GTM needs a wedge. “Prototype slice” satisfies all four better than “game.”

## Elicitation 07 — Critique and Refine
**Question:** What wording in the original opening cluster sounded strong but hid risk?  
**Finding:** “creator-to-production copilot for game development” was directionally right but still too broad. The revised line improves by adding the first real user, first proof shape, and bounded workflow.

## Elicitation 08 — Failure Mode / Contradiction Hunting
**Question:** Where does the current story internally contradict itself?  
**Finding:** The strongest contradiction is engine-flexible thesis vs. launch-level depth. Saying Godot and Unity are equal at day one is not credible, but hiding Unity entirely weakens market reach. This remains an explicit fork to manage.

## Elicitation 09 — Self-Consistency Validation
**Question:** Does the opening cluster match Phase 1 research conclusions?  
**Finding:** Yes, if the wording emphasizes indie technical creators, Godot-first wedge, world-model-as-bounded-ideation, and trust/provenance. No, if it regresses into generic prompt-to-game language.

## Elicitation 10 — JTBD Integrity Check
**Question:** Does the opening actually connect to the paid job-to-be-done?  
**Finding:** It does only when the promise is “editable engine-native prototype this week,” not “make a game.” Buyers pay for saved continuation time and reduced chaos, not for abstract creative magic alone.

## Elicitation 11 — Message Stress Test
**Question:** Which single phrase best survives skeptical developer scrutiny?  
**Winning phrase:** **structured, editable, engine-native prototype slice**  
**Why:** It is specific, tangible, and aligned with the anti-slop position.

## Elicitation 12 — Founder-Risk Lens
**Question:** Which opening framing is least likely to trap the product in the wrong roadmap?  
**Finding:** A creator-to-production copilot for world-driven prototype slices is safer than a generic game-maker framing. It naturally leads to artifact graphs, compile layers, and playtest intelligence rather than endless surface-generation requests.

## Synthesized Decisions from the Elicitation Pass
1. The opening cluster should optimize for **continuation value**, not generation spectacle.
2. The first sentence should speak to a **specific first wedge**, not the entire future category.
3. “Prototype slice” is more strategically honest than “game.”
4. The product vision should preserve ambition, but the opening promise must stay buildable.
5. Engine flexibility remains a thesis-level truth, while Godot-first remains the strongest launch-level answer.
6. Trust/provenance belong in the opening narrative because they directly support continuation and publishing confidence.

## Notable Contradictions Preserved for Later Resolution
- Godot-first go-to-market vs Unity-first revenue gravity
- solo technical creator vs solo/duo team as the primary buyer
- exploration/survival vs action-roguelite as the first proof genre
