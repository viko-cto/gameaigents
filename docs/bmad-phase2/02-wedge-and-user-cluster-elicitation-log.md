# GameAIgents Wedge and User Cluster — Elicitation Log

_Date: 2026-03-19_  
_Status: Completed challenge pass for wedge-and-user cluster_

## Overview
This log records the advanced elicitation pass used to pressure-test the primary user, initial commercial wedge, workflow, and anti-wedges.

## Elicitation 01 — Comparative Segment Ranking
**Question:** Which user segment produces the best combination of pain, adoption tolerance, reachability, and fit with the real product differentiation?  
**Finding:** The solo technical creator wins. Small teams have budget, but also higher workflow expectations. Design-led non-technical users create higher support pressure and push the product toward automation theater.

## Elicitation 02 — Core JTBD Integrity Check
**Question:** Who most urgently needs “structured, editable, engine-native continuation” rather than just idea generation?  
**Finding:** The solo technical creator. This is the segment that feels the blank-page-to-first-playable pain most acutely while still being able to exploit real engine artifacts.

## Elicitation 03 — Willingness-to-Pay vs Support Burden
**Question:** Which segment is most likely to pay without forcing the product into overpromised white-glove behavior?  
**Finding:** Solo technical creators and solo-duo builders remain the healthiest early fit. Non-technical creators may be interested, but their support burden and expectations distort the roadmap.

## Elicitation 04 — Red Team vs Blue Team: “Godot is too niche”
**Red team:** Launching Godot-first makes the product sound small and hobbyist.  
**Blue team response:** Godot-first is a launch-depth choice, not a forever-market ceiling. It creates a sharper, more differentiated proof while keeping Unity visible in the architecture and roadmap.

## Elicitation 05 — Anti-Wedge Stress Test
**Question:** What happens if the product leads with “make any game with AI”?  
**Finding:** It collapses into the most crowded lane in the category, weakens trust with serious developers, and turns the first promise into something neither credible nor strategically differentiated.

## Elicitation 06 — Workflow Survivability Test
**Question:** Which user is most likely to survive a rough-but-real v1 workflow?  
**Finding:** The solo technical creator, because they can inspect outputs, debug issues, replace weak pieces, and still extract value from a real scaffold.

## Elicitation 07 — Cross-Functional War Room
**Participants simulated:** product, engineering, GTM, community, trust/compliance.  
**Finding:** All sides preferred a narrow builder-first wedge. Product wanted a believable first win; engineering wanted a manageable integration surface; GTM wanted a crisp story; trust/compliance wanted anti-slop honesty.

## Elicitation 08 — Founder/Channel Fit Lens
**Question:** Which user can be reached through realistic early distribution channels?  
**Finding:** Solo technical creators match Discord communities, indie dev subreddits, itch.io, game-jam circles, and creator/devlog channels far better than studio procurement paths or mass-consumer no-code funnels.

## Elicitation 09 — Contradiction Hunt: Solo vs Duo
**Question:** Does the wedge break if the real early user is a duo instead of a pure solo builder?  
**Finding:** No. But the copy gets weaker if “duo” becomes the headline. Collaboration should be adjacency, not the primary promise. The product must prove single-user value first.

## Elicitation 10 — Engine Positioning Consistency Test
**Question:** How do we reconcile Godot-first execution with engine-flexible strategy?  
**Finding:** By treating Godot-first as the launch wedge and workflow depth, while keeping Unity visible as a serious follow-on path at the thesis and architecture level.

## Elicitation 11 — Genre / Proof-Shape Test
**Question:** What project type best matches the wedge without overspecifying the whole future product?  
**Finding:** World-driven single-player prototype slices are the strongest first proof shape. They fit the idea cascade + world sketch + compile loop better than multiplayer, service-heavy, or highly system-dense genres.

## Elicitation 12 — Self-Consistency Validation
**Question:** Does the wedge-and-user answer align with Phase 1 research and the opening-cluster decisions?  
**Finding:** Yes. The answer stays consistent with the anti-slop, creator-first, continuation-value thesis and sharpens the launch story without reopening the core strategy.

## Synthesized Decisions from the Elicitation Pass
1. The first true primary user is the **solo technical creator**.
2. **Solo-duo builder** remains in-scope, but as adjacency rather than opening copy.
3. The first commercial wedge should be **Godot-first**, not Godot-only.
4. The first proof unit should be a **world-driven single-player prototype slice**.
5. The workflow must prove **human continuation in-engine**, not just generation speed.
6. The strongest anti-wedges are generic prompt-to-game, browser-first toy creation, non-technical-first automation, and world-model spectacle positioning.

## Notable Contradictions Preserved for Later Resolution
- how explicitly launch messaging should say **Godot-first** versus **engine-flexible**
- how much collaboration support belongs in the first proof versus later expansion
- which exact first genre within the world-driven slice should become the flagship demo
- whether world sketch is required in the first proof or optional when direct compile from brief is sufficient
